                            1.  Purpose

                            This purpose of this SOP is to outline the responsibilities of the L4 Leadership role within the SSD department. 

                            2.  Links/Tools

                            SSD Leadership Portal

                            3.  Responsibilities
                            3.1.  Start of Shift
                            3.1.1.  Duties

                            Start of Shift (SoS)

                            1. Check EoS notes for your cluster to ensure you are tracking any callouts or concerns being handed off from the previous shift. A bot would populate with a message. Example below,

                               (SDC1 team, @LEAD will be your CO POC for the shift. If any support is needed, please feel free to reach out.)

                            2. Copy/paste the following message to your site:
                              "Hello Team, I will be your lead for the shift. Below you can see current risk and the HC inputs being used in OSP. Please reply to this message if you have questions or concerns. Thank you."
                              In addition to the standard message please pull up CORA and SQUIRREL. Take a snip of both the risk and the PDP for the current day.

                               
                            3. When setting up your station ensure that you have the MC Dashboard  set up to monitor your own MC queue (See section 5 for guidance).
                            4. Open the CORA Dashboard and the SSD Caps Dashboard to track sites current risk and available capacity. Continue to monitor throughout your shift.

                            5. Once lead has set-up their computer for the day and ready to start working with assigned cluster. Leads responsibility is to be assigned to the correct cluster in Amazon connect and logging into Mission Control within 15 mins of SOS.  Risk Dashboard

                            3.2.  Conductor Planning
                            3.2.1.  Duties

                            Task execution is Lead owned, it iss the leads responisibility to:

                            1. Ensure Conductor plans are generated and delivered within SLA
                            2. Validate all plan inputs prior to generation window
                            3. Respond and action any pending UTR override DocBots

                            Detailed break down of the entire OSP Planning Policy HERE

                            3.3.  SEV Events
                            3.3.1.  Duties

                            Lead

                            What to do if you encounter a SEV event: 

                            1. You are to follow the SEV2 or SEV1 protocol for any of your cluster sites outlined in the CO SSD LSE SOP
                            2. In person notification to an AM. A slack message is not applicable for possible SEV events
                            1. Submit Lead to Problem Solve DocBot
                            1. Receive notification from UTR that a SEV event is happening. If a SIM was included in UTRs escelation proceed to step 2. If not request that UTR provides one. No further actions should take place until the SIM is provided.
                            2. Once you have a SIM from UTR please submit a Lead to Problem Solve DocBot located in the-ssd-chat. Specific steps for how to do this are located in Section 3.8.1 of this SOP. Be sure to include the SIM provided by UTR.
                            3. Once a problem solver has received your submission they will create and add you to a slack channel to streamline communication with UTR regarding their SEV. Please monitor this room from communication from your UTR partners and the problem solver assisting with this closure. They may ask you to take capping actions or to engage TRB.

                            Problem Solve

                            When a mission control task populates for a SEV Event Lead to Problem Solve Escalation  there are three separate tasks that the problem solver owns.

                            1. Check into the SIM & Create the War Room
                            2. Action SEV Event Protocol. (TRB/Caps) then post the impact.
                            3. Monitor the Situation.
                              Task 1. Check Into the SIM & Create the War Room
                            1 First, open the SEV2 SIM provided by UTR. Click  the "Communication" tab and comment "CO Monitoring" by typing in the text box and clicking "Post to Correspondence"  
                            2

                            Next, open Slack. Click the “+” icon on the bottom Left Side of the window to “Create new”, then select “Channel” in the selection pane.

                            3

                            In the “Create a channel” window Select “Operations” from the drop down, the click “Next”.

                            4

                            Now create the channel name using the following format:

                            -SITE-MONTH-DAY-ISSUE-SEV

                            If the issue is more than one word use dashes to separate each word.

                            -Example: STX2-11-27-power-outage-sev

                            Once the channel is named Click "Next".

                             
                            5 Next select "Public", then Click "Create".
                            6

                            The final step to create the channel is to add people. When the "add People" window populates, Add the following POCs, and Stakeholders.

                            -On Duty CO SSD AMs

                            -The Lead running trhe site

                            -The UTR POC that escalated the SEv event.

                            Once al POCs and Stakeholders are in the box Click "Add".
                            7

                            Now, post this script or similar verbiageto inform the recently added POCs and Stakeholders what this room is and why theyve been added to it.

                            “@here This channel was created to streamline communication between CO and UTR regarding the ongoing SEV. Please direct all related communications here.” 

                            Next post the link to the Primary SIM.

                            Then @ the UTR POC who escalated the SEV and post this script or similar verbiage asking them to add any additional UTR Partners they want to the channel.

                            "@POC LOGIN Please add any additional UTR POCs to this room  as needed."

                            The room is now created. Proceed to task 2 to Action standard SEV protocol.

                               Task 2. Action SEV Protocol & Post the Impact
                             

                            Action SEV Event protocol. Current SEV2 Protocol is as follows:

                            TRB the Current and Next window & cap the 3rd and 4th window out by 50%
                            1 Utilize Lagrange overrides to engage TRB for the current and next window.
                            2 Once TRB is active update the war room with this script or similar verbiage along with a snip of the CORA dashboard showing active TRB. ”@here Per SW, the current and next windows have been placed in TRB. Tracking CORA reflects. The 3rd and 4th CPTs will receive a 50% Atrops cap.”
                            3

                            Next Submit a SIM for capping actions to the 3rd and 4th windows Via THIS template and post the SIM in the slack channel for tracking.
                            4

                            Once the SIM is created and posted, action the caps. Detailed instructions on capping via Limelight can be found on the CO SSD Last Mile Capacity Reduction SOP - Limelight. Update the chat once caps are in place and let them know the timeline expected for caps to reflect.
                            5

                            Now, Post the impact Including date, CPT, amount capped and total capped amount using the following format.  

                            @here Current capping impact is as follows:

                            (DATE), (CPT). Capped 50%. (ORIGINAL ATROPS) to (NEW CAP). Amount Capped, (AMNOUNT CAPPED).
                            (DATE), (CPT). Capped 50%. (ORIGINAL ATROPS) to (NEW CAP). Amount Capped, (AMNOUNT CAPPED).

                            Total Amount Capped- (TOTAL AMOUNT CAPPED)

                            Example:

                            @here Current capping impact is as follows:

                            11/12 18:20. Capped 50%. 10000 down to 5000. Amount capped, 5000.

                            11/13 00:20. Capped 50%. 7000 down to 3500. Amount capped, 3500.

                            Total Amount Capped – 8500
                            6 Lastly return to the SEV2 SIM provided by UTR and report out the impact there as well by typing in the text box and clicking "Post to Correspondence"
                              Task 3. Monitor the Call/ Room.
                            1 1. Once Tasks one and two are comploete continue to monitor the chat and the SEV2 SIM for any updates or requests from UTR. If an SSD AM has started a call monitor the line for any questions or call outs from UTR..

                             

                            3.4.  Communication
                            3.4.1.  Duties
                            1. This includes but is not limited to multiple aspects such as misses within the following areas. MC, UFP, Communication with sites .
                            2. CO to site escalation is no greater then 10 minutes
                            3.5.  Ownership
                            3.5.1.  Duties
                            1. You are to ensure that communication methods are met within SLA time frame of 10 minutes between CO and UTR
                            2. Ensure that you are in all the required slack rooms:
                              1. the-ssd-chat – All CO SSD: Class F, Class X, AM, OM, Sr OM
                              2. ssd-lightning-alert – Notifications for Lightning Alerts
                              3. ssd-cf-cfh-escalations-and-support – Escalations for Floor Health
                              4. ssd-co-change-announcements – Thread to see the Change Management
                            3.6.  Lunches & Breaks
                            3.6.1.  Duties

                            We should be utilizing the Break and Lunch workflows located in the ssd chat.

                            When stepping off the floor for a Break or Lunch please use the corresponding workflow to inform your UTR partners that you will be unavailable, for how long and how to escalate should they need something while you are gone.

                             

                                1.  Leads will need to utilize the Risk Dashboard when stepping off the floor from their assigned cluster during breaks and lunches. When placing Handoff through SSD Dashboard there will be a comment box to utilize for proper communication to site and CO SSD team via Slack.

                            (SDC1 team, @LEAD

                             will be on lunch for 30 minutes and will action any pending requests once returning. For any High priority escalations please utilize the CO escalation workflow for an immediate response from CO leadership.)

                                 

                            2.  Please inform all of your sites and the-ssd-chat once you have returned by selecting "Return from Lunch/Break" button from the Risk Dashboard.

                             

                            3.7.  End of Shift (EoS)
                            3.7.1.  Duties

                            EoS (End of Shift):

                            1. You are to ensure that you and your associate are reflecting “Offline” Mission Control CO leads SOP
                            2. You are to ensure that you execute the end of shift workflow in the main SSD chat
                            3. Ensure that the (EoS) workflow includes any issues involving capping, yard closures, active lightning, SEVs etc. with an in-depth description to set up next shift for success.

                            ­­
                            EOS Links:

                            1.  Script Download - https://axzile.corp.amazon.com/-/carthamus/download_script/cora-dashboard.user.js
                            2.  Condensed Dashboard - https://outboundflow-iad.amazon.com/tinydashboard
                            3. Risk Dashboard - https://outboundflow-iad.amazon.com/dashboard 

                            4. Leads are to ensure they’re “Offline” and utilizing the EOS in the SSD Dashboard when finished with shift and leaving for the day. When using the Handoff through SSD Dashboard leads will need to leave a comment (Capping, Yard Closures, SEVs etc.) for proper communication to site and next CO SSD team member. 

                              (SDC1 team, @LEADis now signing out for EOS. Your next CO lead will be online in approximately 30 minutes. For any high priority escalations during this time, please use the CO escalation workflow for an immediate response from CO leadership.)

                               

                            3.8.  Escalation Pathways

                            If your site is not responding to an OSP plan that been sent to be reviewed please follow the steps outlined below: 

                            1. If a site has not responded in 10 minutes from initial request - The next action would be to check OSP history. Copy login from previous acceptance and utilize phone tool and tag that login in the respective chat 

                            2. If a site has not responded within 10 minutes from the first POC escalation (Step #1). The next escalation point would be to go back to phone tool and identify the OM's login and tag their login in the chat 

                            3. If site has not responded within 10 minutes of following step #2 please use the escalation workflow to the on duty AM. 

                            3.8.1.  Lead to Problem Solve Escelations

                            For instances where the help of a problem solver is required please utilize the Lead to PS Escalation DocBot by following the steps outlined below.

                            Lead Process for DocBot Submission.
                            1. Navigate to the-ssd-chat. In the text field type "/docbot", then hit "Send Now" in the bottom right hand corner of your Slack window.
                            2. The DocBot window will populate, choose the "Lead to PS Escalation" form from the dropdown list and hit "Submit".
                            3.Fill in all provided fields including: Login, site, issue, actions taken and applicable links. Then select "Send to Atlas Immidiately" and "Submit". A MC task will be send to a problem solver prompting them to follow up and assist.
                            4. When a problem Solver has receiveed your task a message will populate in the ssd chat showing the new status as "INITIALIZED". A Problem Solver will now review your request, deep dive and provide assistance where able.

                             

                            Problem Solve Process for Responding to Submission.
                            1. When a DocBot subission is received it will populate in the Problem Solve MC queue along with in the-ssd-chat.

                            2. Review submission details provided by the lead, perfom related deep dive and assist lead as able.
                            3. Once complete Click "Done" to clear the task from Mission Control.

                             

                            4.  How to use Mission Control

                            The following sections will detail usage for Mission Control throughout your shift

                            4.1.  Mission Control at SoS

                            1. Open up Mission Control and click on the button next to "Hello" on the top right to toggle on to Mission Control or click on the "I'm Available" button.

                            2. Once you click on either option the button on the top right Mission Control will great you with a message, the button will be colored in with a check mark reading I'm available and your Active Exceptions will populate on the left side. This is where you will recieve your tasks during your shift. Example below:

                            How to set up Mission Control for user profiles:

                            Follow these steps when setting yourself and your associate to the correct cluster

                            1. Navigate to:  Amazon Connect NA
                            2. Hover over the “User” in the top left corner of the screen and select “User management”

                               
                            3.  Next, select "Login" and type the desired login needed, then press the "Add" button and apply the filter
                               
                            4. Now check mark the box next to the login you wish to adjust and select “Edit” in the top right corner of the screen
                               
                            5. Change the routing profile and click the "Save" button in the top right corner.

                            ** Lead profile will be: "SSDLead(1-8)" depending on which cluster you are watching. EX. SSDLead5 **

                              ** Associate profile will be: SSDAssociateCL(1-8) depending on which cluster. EX. SSDAssociateCL6 **

                             

                            4.2.  Mission Control Monitoring

                            How to set up Mission Control for monitoring:
                                1. Please hover over "Analytics and optimization" and select "Dashboards and Reports" in the top left-hand corner

                            1. Next select "Real-time metrics" then “SSD Dashboard”

                            1. Last, we will select SSD Dashboard. From here we can see who is logged in, how long they have been in the profile, which cluster they are assigned to and which exceptions are in their queue.

                            4.3.  Mission control at EoS

                            1. EOS just simply Click the button on the top right and it will toggle you off. The screen will look like the SOS before going available. This is very crucial and a standard that needs to be executed by all leads before they leave for the day. For accurate HC and to make sure all tasks are flowing to their respective lead running the cluster. If not this will get stuck in your queue until it times out possibly putting the SLA at risk.

                             

                            How to set up Mission Control for End of Shift:
                                1. Please hover over "Analytics and optimization" and select "Dashboards and reports" in the top left-hand corner

                            1. Next select "Real-time metrics" then “SSD Dashboard”

                                3. Now find the profile you wish to audit and select the 3 dots to the right of the profile and select “view agents”

                              1. Lastly, ensure you are looking at the right profile by confirming the login to the left, once you have please select “On contact” under the Activity and click the “Offline”. If profile already displpays "Offline" status no additional action is needed.

                            #

                            Step

                            Screenshot

                            1

                             

                            SSD-CO receives Mission Control exception when yard reopens

                             

                            SSD CO monitors MC for exception that yard has reopens. An alert will also appear in the SSD Yard Monitor Slack channel.

                             

                            Note: the MC exception and slack alert will have a link to navigate directly to the CapIT tool.

                            2

                            Run CapIT Tool

                            Once the yard is reopened, the Mission Control / SSD Yard Monitor alert will display a link within the alert that will direct SSD-CO directly to the CapIT tool. CO clicks the link to open the "Cap IT" tool and the following inputs will be auto-populated:
                            1. Input site code
                            2. Input closure start date / time
                            3. Input closure end date / time
                            4. Click submit

                             

                            3

                            		 Capacity Adjustment

                            ​​​​​​​

                            1. Review "Pending Packages" in CapIT to determine the amount of packages needed to cap down by in Limelight

                             - For SSD-Core: If package count is less than 300, no capping action should be taken 

                            FOR SSD-Direct sites we are not using CapIT toolL or capping actions Refer to Section 6 for SSD-D SOP.

                             

                            2. If current window doesn't have room to cap by, apply the amount that can't be capped in current window to next available window. If additional caps are still needed, apply to subsequent future windows as well.

                                -Ex: Current window has only 2500 units left to max cap --> apply 2500 to current and 1005 units to the next available window

                            Note: If there are already proactive Myst caps in place due to forecasted weather, please compare Myst caps for future windows with CapIT output and use the larger of the two values.

                             

                            4

                            Log Capacity Adjustment

                            Submit a SIM using this template  to log any capacity adjustment completed. 

                            Select reason code as "Weather - Yard Closure". PLEASE DO NOT SKIP THIS STEP.

                            **PLEASE ALSO POST THE LINK FOR THE CAPPING SIM in the Lightning Opening Alert**

                            Comment on the SSD Yard Monitor Re-Open slack alert with a snippet of the full Cap IT tool page showing the following:
                            -Volume capped

                            **PLEASE ALSO POST THE LINK FOR THE MANUAL ADD SIM in the Lightning Opening Alert**
                            5.2.  Collate Step Process

                            4.1.  Manual Collate

                            Occasionally shipments will get stuck in Condition 29(C29)/ready to pick (RTP) and will not collate into condition 4(C4)/Picking not yet picked (PNYP). Some SSD sites will request help from CO when this happens.

                            4.1.1.  Links

                            1. Collate UI
                            2. Rodeo

                                   When opening these links, update the site name in the URL to match the site you are collating for

                            4.1.2.  Process

                            1.  If a shipment ID is not provided when the request to collate is sent by UTR, you can find it by viewing "Split Demands" process path in rodeo.

                                            a. Click on the total number for your CPT

                                

                                b. Copy the first shipment ID

                                

                            2. On Collate UI, click on "Collate a Shipment" and paste your shipment ID

                            3. Click "Collate" and a success message will appear
